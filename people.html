The Locals

The people of Batac City, known as Batacqueños, are predominantly Ilocanos, sharing in the wider cultural identity of Ilocos Norte and the Ilocos Region. They are widely recognized for their industry, resilience, and strong sense of community. Like most Ilocanos, the people of Batac are known for being hardworking farmers, traders, and entrepreneurs, qualities that have helped sustain the city through centuries of social and economic changes.


The population of Batac is a mix of both urban and rural communities. While many residents are engaged in agriculture such as planting rice, corn, and tobacco others pursue livelihoods in education, trade, and government service. Batac is home to Mariano Marcos State University (MMSU), which attracts students and professionals, making education and research an important part of local life.


Culturally, Batacqueños maintain strong traditions in family ties, respect for elders, and Christian faith, with Catholicism being the dominant religion. The city is also known for its vibrant festivities, most notably the Empanada Festival, which showcases Batac’s culinary heritage. Beyond this, Batac is often called the “Home of Great Leaders”, as it is the birthplace of national figures such as General Artemio Ricarte and former President Ferdinand E. Marcos, reflecting the city’s contribution to Philippine history and leadership.
